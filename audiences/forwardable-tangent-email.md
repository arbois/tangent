A friend of mine is setting up Tangent (https://tangent.org.sg) — a privately funded, unconventional entrepreneurial incubator for young people in Singapore aged 18–21 (18–22 for men with NS obligations) who want to build AI-native organisations (whether for-profit businesses or not-for-profit/pro-social enterprises). It starts with a four-week sprint: S$1,000 stipend, AI tools, and human guides to work on their own ideas — no coursework, no assignments. Participants present results at the end of four weeks; the commitment ends there. The ones who show genuine promise can continue into a 24-month fellowship with up to S$200,000 in funding.

The profile they're looking for: self-directed and motivated, but not necessarily the top academic performer — more likely the person with an idea they keep talking about, or who's restless within the conventional system and looking for a different path.

1. If you know someone who fits this description, please refer them or encourage them to apply directly — details at https://tangent.org.sg.

2. If you'd be willing to drop in as a guide — two hours once per cohort, available to participants on demand, no prep required — please get in touch and I'll connect you. If you know someone who would be good in this role, please pass this on to them.

3. If you know others who work closely with young people in this age range and would be well-placed to refer candidates, please forward this to them.
