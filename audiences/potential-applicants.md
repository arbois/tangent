I wanted to make sure you know about a programme I think might be right for you — or for someone you know.

Tangent is a 24-month programme for young people in Singapore who want to build something on their own terms. This could be a for-profit business, a social enterprise, a not-for-profit, or anything that could eventually make them a good living. Participants receive up to S$200,000 each over two years to develop their idea and bring it to market.

Tangent starts with a four-week programme. You get S$1,000, access to AI tools, and human guides who are there to help you think — not to tell you what to do. There are no mandatory courses, no required assignments. You define the work you want to do, do it, and show us what you found at the end of four weeks. Your commitment ends there if you want it to, or you can pitch to continue into the 24-month programme. (Several universities have already agreed to defer matriculation for participants.)

We don't require you to submit grades or a polished pitch to apply for the four weeks. You don't need to be highly technical (or even a bit technical) to apply, nor do you need to have a fully developed idea to apply. Age range 18–21 (or 18–22 for men with NS obligations), but we can be flexible on that.

https://tangent.org.sg/ has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, and how to refer someone you know.
