I wanted to make sure you know about a programme I think might be right for you — or for someone you know.

Tangent is a 24-month programme for young people in Singapore who want to build something on their own terms — a business, a social enterprise, a not-for-profit, or anything that could eventually make them a good living. Participants receive up to S$200,000 each over two years to develop their idea and bring it to market.

It starts with a four-week programme. You get S$1,000, access to AI tools, and human guides who are there to help you think — not to tell you what to do. No mandatory courses, no assignments. You define the work, do it, and show Tangent what you found at the end of four weeks. Your commitment ends there if you want it to. Several universities have already agreed to defer matriculation for participants. If you want to continue, you pitch for the full 24-month programme.

No required grades, no polished pitch to apply for the four weeks. Age range 18–21 (or 18–22 for men with NS obligations).

https://tangent.org.sg/ has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, how to refer someone you know, and how to get involved as a guide.
