## Email — for model and hardware companies (direct outreach)

*Direct email to developer relations or partnerships teams at foundation model companies and hardware providers.*

---

I'm building a new AI applications incubator in Singapore called Tangent (tangent.org.sg). It's a 24-month fellowship — up to S$200,000 per participant — for young Singapore-based entrepreneurs building AI-native businesses. Privately funded through the Majurity Trust; no commercial relationship with any model or hardware provider.

We start with a four-week programme: participants get S$1,000 and access to AI tools and human guides to work on their own ideas. No courses, no assignments. Just structured time to build something real. The ones who show the most promise continue into the 24-month fellowship.

The people we're looking for are 18–21 (18–22 for men who've completed NS) — unconventional, early, not the profiles that show up at Antler or NUS Enterprise. They want to build AI-native businesses and haven't had access to the tools or environment to do it yet.

Three things I'd like to ask for:

1. **Donated or subsidised API access** — credits or subscriptions so participants can build without worrying about inference costs during the programme
2. **Donated or subsidised hardware** — for local inference, for participants whose ideas need it
3. **Occasional guide time** — one or two people willing to be in the programme space for two hours, once per cohort, available to participants on demand. Not structured mentoring. Just being present and accessible.

Why this might be worth it: the people coming through Tangent are building AI-native from day one. The tools they get comfortable with here are the tools they'll keep building on. This isn't a hackathon or demo day — participants are working on real ideas with real time and real resources. Early and genuine, not performative.

I'm the founding executive director of Tangent, a Fellow at Singapore's Centre for Strategic Futures, ex-Google, and honorary associate professor in strategy and entrepreneurship at UCL. More at vaughntan.org and linkedin.com/in/vaughntan.

Would you be up for a 20-minute call?
