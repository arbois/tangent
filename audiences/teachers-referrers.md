I wanted to make sure you know about a programme I think could use your help finding the right candidates.

Tangent is a 24-month programme providing up to S$200,000 per participant for young people in Singapore who want to build something on their own terms. The right candidate doesn't have to be your top academic student, doesn't have to be hyper-technical (or even a little bit technical), nor do they have to have a very refined idea to work on — it's more likely to be the one with the seed of idea they keep talking about, or the one who is motivated and self-directed but restless within the conventional system.

Tangent starts with a four-week programme: S$1,000, access to AI tools, and human guides and a wide range of mentoring options. There is no mandatory coursework, no compulsory assignments. Participants self-define the work they want to do on their idea (Tangent staff will help them clarify and refine their ideas) and show us their results at the end of four weeks. There are no further obligations. Those who want to can pitch to continue into the longer programme, and several universities have already agreed to defer matriculation for those participants. Our target age range is 18–21 for women and men without NS obligations, and 18–22 for men with NS obligations — but we are flexible for applicants who excite us.

So if you know a current student or alumnus who belongs in Tangent, please refer them. And if you know other teachers or referrers who should know about this, please pass it on.

https://tangent.org.sg/ has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, how to refer someone, and how to get involved as a guide.
