A friend of mine is setting up Tangent (https://tangent.org.sg) — a privately funded, unconventional entrepreneurial incubator for young people in Singapore aged 18–22 who want to build businesses, not-for-profits, or social enterprises that are informed by AI. It starts with a four-week sprint: each participant gets a S$1,000 stipend, access to AI tools, and human guides to work on their own ideas. Participants present results at the end of four weeks; the commitment ends there. Those who show promise can continue into a 24-month fellowship with up to S$200,000 in funding.

The profile Tangent is looking for: self-directed and motivated people, but not necessarily top academic performers. They're looking for people with an idea they keep talking about, or who are restless within the conventional system and looking for a different path.

Three ways you could help:

1. If you know someone who fits this description, please refer them or encourage them to apply directly — details at https://tangent.org.sg.
2. If you'd be willing to drop in as a guide — two hours once per cohort, available to participants on demand, no prep required — please get in touch and I'll connect you. If you know someone who would be good in this role, please pass this on to them.
3. If you know others who work closely with young people in this age range and would be well-placed to refer candidates, please forward this to them.
