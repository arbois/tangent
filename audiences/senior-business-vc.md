I wanted to make sure you know about a programme I think is worth your attention — and ask if you'd be willing to help.

Tangent is a 24-month programme providing up to S$200,000 for young people in Singapore — aged 18–21, or 18–22 for men with NS obligations — who are self-directed and motivated but whose potential doesn't show up in grades or credentials. They're the ones Singapore's existing incubators and accelerators structurally can't reach.

It starts with a four-week programme: S$1,000, AI tools, and human guides. No mandatory courses, no assignments. Participants define their own work and show results at the end of four weeks. Those who want to continue pitch for the full 24-month programme. The programme is also open to anyone who wants to pitch, whether or not they've done the four weeks.

If you'd be willing to help in any of these ways:
- Refer a young person who belongs in Tangent
- Come in as a guide for two hours during a cohort — available to participants, not lecturing
- Support the programme with resources

https://tangent.org.sg/ has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, how to refer someone, and how to get involved as a guide.
